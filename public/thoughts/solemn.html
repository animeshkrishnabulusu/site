A wish

Jan 5, 2013

With all that happened recently, I could not bring myself to wish anyone I knew personally a New Year wholeheartedly. I do not think someone would be offended that their wish wasn't reciprocated.

I sincerely wish serious some changes about us.

By the end of 2012, India has come into focus of the world. This time, it was for something that has been happening for a long time, but went unnoticed or unreported - gang-rapes, among other atrocities against women.

Along with the rest of the world, we Indians learnt of how some depraved individuals committed one of the most heinous acts in the history of world.

I wish, instead, that people start realizing that it is completely okay for "The Mera the Bharat the Mahan" to contain negative shades and elements, and that it is okay to talk about it and others to know about it. We all should commit to change it, one step at a time, toward the better society we ought to be.

When hundreds of people all over the country protested over the cruel acts, government inaction and inefficiency, I could feel on my skin a ray of hope, a light at the end of the tunnel that suggested that, we the people of India are not complete vegetables yet, that we are not dead yet.

A nation, full of callous, cynical, and resigned individuals, has had time to ponder about the atrocities and realized that it is within them is the mark of change.

I wish, instead, that more and more people take to protests and activism and show the power of collectivism to everything that is wrong about this country.

The fact that we still have millions of us who say, think and believe "Well, yeah, she was asking for it" or "she was provocatively dressed" or "that was bound to happen sooner or later" confirms and reaffirms that we have a long way to go in terms of a safe society.

It is unnerving to listen to people with daughters saying that the girl deserves humiliation by proxy if she is dressed contemporarily or is modern. It is a damn shame on people of who can even think that.

I wish, instead, that all Indians, including the know-it-alls, the helplessly materialistic, the shameless powers that be, the cynics and the helpless, to realize that it is within them to bring forward the change.

Know, and let people know, that 2013 is a year of a social and a personal awakening. Make the year 2013 all about seeing and making this change within oneself and others around you.
