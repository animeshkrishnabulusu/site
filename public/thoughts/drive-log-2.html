Drive Log 2

Rehabilitation of mind

Nov 11, 2012

Today I woke up from an odd dream. I wondered for a while why the dream did not continue despite no external disturbances. I then made myself ready for a drive.

We went toward the IT park like the last time.

I feared I would feel as if I were starting from scratch, since the last drive was fifteen days ago. I was mildly surprised that I did not feel intimidated by all the gear shifting that was to come ahead.

This time I had fewer hiccups on the road, be it U-turns or slowing down when it needed. I sensed the driver in me was coming back. I was reminded of how much I used to dig driving while I was in the States.

I ventured a bit further onto the main road this time, toward Lingampally. I kept going. After missing a traffic light, I began being careful. I drove for a bit on the road toward Mehdipatnam and then decided to turn toward Hitech city.

I realized I would encounter my "first" traffic light in a long time. I slowed down as I approached the junction and came to a halt. I did not get down to neutral as I was supposed to. I was holding clutch all the way down in second gear and waited for the green.

As it would be, a lot of vehicles piled up behind me. Suddenly I was overcome by the thought of not being able to accelerate appropriately and getting stuck right where I stood. I could hear my own heartbeat and waited nervously for the signal. The moment the signal turned green, my heart was racing. I was still in second gear. I released the clutch and accelerated a bit and moved a bit forward. Not having accelerated enough, I could hear the dreaded sounds coming from the vehicle indicating an impending and unconvenient halt.

At that moment, I don't really recollect what drove me further, but neither did I panic and step on the brakes, nor did I accelerate out of control. I kept going on and that actually worked. Yeah, I had no reason to panic. I am not new to the roads and controlled acceleration was something I could do without much effort.

I was able to steer out of the junction and move ahead. I gave up a bit further down the road citing the driving being a bit more "stressful" than on low-traffic roads.

Though my father was silent for the most part, I could see that he was amply irked. He mentioned something about becoming more confident only if I drive in the "real" traffic. He was right.

I am hoping to do that next time.
